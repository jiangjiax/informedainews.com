General Motors (GM) electric vehicles can now use Tesla's Superchargers, thanks to a new adapter. This move expands the North American Charging Standard (NACS) compatibility, easing concerns about charging options for GM customers. The adapter, priced at $225, grants access to roughly 17,800 Tesla Superchargers, boosting GM's total fast-charger network to 231,800 across the U.S.

GM is also part of IONNA, a group planning to build 30,000 high-powered chargers nationwide. This partnership marks a significant shift, as GM and Tesla were once fierce rivals, particularly over Tesla's direct sales model. Now, cooperation benefits drivers, offering reliable, fast, and convenient charging.
