New research indicates that administering immunotherapy both before and after lung cancer surgery significantly lowers the risk of death and disease recurrence. Immunotherapy, a treatment that harnesses the body's immune system to fight cancer, has shown promising results in recent studies.

The study, published in a reputable medical journal, suggests that patients who undergo this dual approach experience better outcomes compared to those who receive surgery alone. The pre-surgery immunotherapy primes the immune system, while post-surgery treatment helps prevent the cancer from returning.

This method not only extends patients' lives but also improves their quality of life by reducing the likelihood of the disease spreading. The findings are a significant step forward in cancer treatment, offering hope to those diagnosed with lung cancer.
