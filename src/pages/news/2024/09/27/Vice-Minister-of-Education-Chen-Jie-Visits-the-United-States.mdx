Vice Minister of Education Chen Jie led a delegation to the United States, engaging in in-depth exchanges with U.S. officials to promote Sino-U.S. educational cooperation. The two sides reached multiple agreements, including establishing a regular dialogue mechanism, advancing the "5-Year, 50,000" initiative, deepening cooperation in higher and basic education, and enhancing collaboration on students' physical and mental health. Chen Jie also participated in a forum with presidents of top universities from both countries, visited educational institutions, and held discussions with Chinese scholars, teachers, and international students. This visit marks a positive recovery in Sino-U.S. educational exchanges.

**Notes**:
- **"5-Year, 50,000" Initiative**: Refers to a plan where both countries aim to facilitate the exchange of 50,000 students within five years.
