Microsoft and Activision are forming a new team at Blizzard, focusing on "2A" game development, such as "World of Warcraft" and "Overwatch." This move is part of Microsoft's strategic expansion following its acquisition of Activision Blizzard, aiming to maintain and enhance the vitality of these popular game series. The new team may include mobile game development, particularly optimizing "Diablo Immortal." Additionally, the team might be involved in developing new mobile games for the Xbox mobile game store, which will support iOS and Android platforms. Microsoft prefers using small, flexible teams for innovation, which can later integrate into larger projects. Currently, the 2A game market is performing strongly, with titles like "Hades" and "Stardew Valley" enjoying widespread popularity.
