Cloud Learning, a leading digital training provider in China, has recently listed on NASDAQ under the ticker YXT. Their domain, YTX.com, mirrors their name and history since 2000. They specialize in corporate training through SaaS and digital learning services, catering to major clients such as Fortune 500 companies.

Their IPO was priced at $11 per share, with the goal of raising $25 million. The funds will be used to enhance R&D, technology, and brand visibility. Lu Ruizhe, the founder, holds significant voting power, ensuring strategic stability.

The listing strengthens their global market presence. With new capital, they plan to improve R&D, technology, and brand influence, driving innovation and market expansion in digital learning solutions.

Cloud Learning's debut on NASDAQ marks a critical milestone in their growth and global recognition. They aim to continue delivering top-tier learning solutions to corporate clients, advancing the industry.

Other notable Chinese firms with three-letter .com domains include BYD, Amazon Web Services, Johnson & Johnson, IBM, Apple, Jingdong Logistics, Vipshop, and China Construction Bank.
