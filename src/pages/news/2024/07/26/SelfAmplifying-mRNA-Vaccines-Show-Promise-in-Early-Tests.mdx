Self-amplifying mRNA vaccines have cleared initial safety tests in labs and animals. These vaccines employ a modified form of messenger RNA that directs cells to generate a protein, stimulating an immune response. The "self-amplifying" feature implies that the mRNA duplicates itself, potentially enhancing the immune response while requiring less material.

The tests indicate these vaccines are safe, with no severe side effects noted. This technology could revolutionize vaccine efficacy, enabling more potent vaccines with reduced doses. It marks a promising advancement in vaccine development, particularly in addressing future pandemics.
