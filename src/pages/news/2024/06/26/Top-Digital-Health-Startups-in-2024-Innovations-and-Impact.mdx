In 2024, digital health investment remains tempered, with Q1 deals totaling $1 billion, a modest 3% rise. Yet, select startups capture investor interest. Abridge, an AI-driven medical note-taking service, stands out, integrating with Epic Systems to streamline physician documentation. CodaMetrix automates medical coding, crucial for reducing errors and administrative strain. Cohere Health accelerates insurance approvals, saving hours in prior authorization processes.

Grow Therapy supports independent therapists, offering a 'business-in-box' model for practice management. Equip delivers online treatment for eating disorders, addressing a significant unmet need. Maven focuses on women's health, a sector gaining attention post-Roe v. Wade. Memora Health uses AI for virtual care coordination, easing staff burdens and enhancing patient support.

SmarterDx ensures accurate medical coding to maximize hospital revenues. Summer Health provides rapid pediatrician responses to parental health queries. Transcarent aids large employers in managing health benefits, leveraging AI for personalized coverage insights.

These startups, diverse in focus but united in leveraging technology to improve healthcare efficiency and access, reflect a sector evolving to meet post-COVID challenges.
