The 2024 Nobel Prize in Economics was awarded to MIT Professor Daron Acemoglu, Simon Johnson, and University of Chicago Professor James A. Robinson.

They were recognized for their in-depth research on the formation of institutions and their impact on prosperity.

Acemoglu, a professor in the Department of Economics at MIT, specializes in macroeconomics and political economy.

He is the author of bestsellers "The Narrow Corridor: States, Societies, and the Fate of Liberty" and "Why Nations Fail: The Origins of Power, Prosperity, and Poverty."

The Nobel Prize in Economics is a high recognition of their academic contributions.
