President Joe Biden withdraws from the 2024 election, endorses Kamala Harris. He remains in office until January. Republicans demand his immediate resignation.

Harris isn't a shoo-in. Delegates at the Democratic National Convention (DNC) determine the nominee. Biden's endorsement enhances her prospects. No formidable challengers emerge.

Potential candidates like Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer opt out of running. Others unite behind Harris. The DNC confronts an open convention, a rare occurrence.

Delegates cast their votes. If no majority is reached, superdelegates participate in the ballot. 1,976 votes are needed to secure the nomination.

Harris's choice for VP is less formal. Potential selections include Shapiro, Pritzker, and Kelly.

Biden's $100 million campaign funds may transfer to Harris. Republicans threaten legal action. The fate of the funds remains uncertain if a different nominee arises.
