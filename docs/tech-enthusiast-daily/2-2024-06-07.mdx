The test aimed to scrutinize Starship's resilience during the most brutal part of atmospheric re-entry, a phase crucial for any spacecraft aiming to return from space safely.

### Gene Therapy Restores Hearing in Deaf Children: A Medical Breakthrough

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/07/Te-4zj-1717724925-0.jpg"
  alt="Gene Therapy Restores Hearing in Deaf Children: A Medical Breakthrough"
/>

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/07/Te-wZW-1717724925-1.jpg"
  alt="Gene Therapy Restores Hearing in Deaf Children: A Medical Breakthrough"
/>

Gene therapy has restored hearing in five deaf children, aged one to eleven, in a groundbreaking trial. These children, born with a genetic condition called DFNB9, lacked a protein essential for hearing. The therapy, developed by researchers at Fudan University and Harvard's Massachusetts Eye and Ear, uses a harmless virus to deliver a functional gene, Otof, into the inner ear. This enables the production of the missing protein, otoferlin, crucial for auditory signals.

Within weeks of treatment, the children could locate sounds and understand speech, even in noisy environments. Video evidence showed a two-year-old boy responding to his name and dancing to music, activities he previously ignored. The oldest patient, an 11-year-old girl, progressed from no response to tones to participating in speech training.

This therapy not only offers hope for children with DFNB9 but also opens possibilities for treating other forms of deafness, whether genetic or not. However, larger trials are needed to fully assess the therapy's benefits and risks, particularly the immune response triggered by the viral delivery method.

The implications of this breakthrough are profound, potentially transforming the lives of millions affected by hearing loss. It marks a significant step towards personalized medicine, where treatments are tailored to the genetic makeup of individuals, offering a new era in the treatment of deafness.

In 1999, Napster emerged, sparking a digital revolution. This file-sharing platform, conceived by young Shawn Fanning, enabled users to freely exchange music files, contrasting sharply with the lucrative CD industry. Napster's influence was significant; it challenged traditional music sales while also laying the groundwork for contemporary streaming services like Spotify and Netflix.

MP3 technology, which compressed music files with minimal loss in quality, was instrumental in this transformation. Developed by German engineers, it facilitated the storage of music on digital devices, paving the way for Napster's ascent.

Despite legal challenges that ultimately led to Napster's closure, the concept of file sharing had become widespread. Other platforms such as Grokster and BitTorrent carried on the legacy. Presently, the music industry flourishes through streaming, a model indirectly influenced by Napster's revolutionary impact.

Napster, though now a historical artifact, remains a crucial milestone in the evolution of digital media consumption. Its legacy underscores the transformative power of technology in reshaping industries and consumer behaviors.
