### Exploring eSIM Technology for Cost-Effective Travel Data Solutions

For travelers, eSIMs offer a modern solution to avoid hefty roaming fees. Unlike traditional SIM cards, eSIMs are embedded into newer phones, simplifying the process of switching carriers and managing multiple networks. This digital technology allows users to add a local data plan from a virtual carrier with competitive rates, directly from their phone, eliminating the need for physical swaps.

The market for eSIMs is thriving, with numerous providers offering various data packages at competitive prices. For instance, Airalo and Holafly provide options tailored to different travel needs and durations. Activation typically involves scanning a QR code or entering a code, a process detailed by phone manufacturers and eSIM providers.

Notably, over 200 devices support eSIM technology, including recent models from Apple, Samsung, and Google. However, before opting for an eSIM, it's wise to compare your carrier's roaming costs with eSIM rates, as some carriers have reduced their roaming fees significantly.

In essence, eSIMs streamline international connectivity, making travel less about managing SIM cards and more about enjoying the journey.

### Rapid UTI Test Wins Longitude Prize for Speedy Detection and Treatment

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/14/Te-6rB-1718329056-0.jpg"
  alt="Rapid UTI Test Wins Longitude Prize for Speedy Detection and Treatment"

  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/14/Te-MI2-1718329056-1.jpg"
  alt="Rapid UTI Test Wins Longitude Prize for Speedy Detection and Treatment"
/>

A rapid UTI test, capable of identifying treatment in 45 minutes, has won the ¬£8m Longitude prize. Developed by Sysmex Astrego, this test utilizes a urine sample to detect bacterial infection within 15 minutes and recommends an antibiotic within 45 minutes. Traditional lab tests typically take two to three days.

The impact of this test is significant, addressing the critical issue of antimicrobial resistance (AMR), where antibiotics lose their effectiveness. AMR is responsible for nearly 1.3 million deaths annually and is projected to rise to 10 million by 2050.

Currently, doctors often prescribe antibiotics based on symptoms, leading to overuse and resistance. This new test enables precise antibiotic prescription, potentially reducing resistance and enhancing patient care.

Although currently priced at ¬£25 per cartridge, the test has the potential to become more affordable with increased production and public sector adoption. This could revolutionize UTI treatment globally, especially in low-resource settings.

The Longitude prize winner not only represents a technological advancement but also a strategic shift in antibiotic management, moving from 'just-in-case' prescribing to targeted, effective treatment.

The team expects to discover even older seagrass clones, potentially ranking them as the oldest living organisms on Earth. This research highlights the effectiveness of interdisciplinary collaboration in advancing ecological and evolutionary science.
