## DJI's Affordable Driver Assistance Tech Set to Transform Automotive Industry

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/06/AI-l39-1717673902-0.jpg"
  alt="DJI's Affordable Driver Assistance Tech Set to Transform Automotive Industry"
/>

DJI Automotive, a spin-off from the drone giant DJI, is transforming the affordability of driver assistance technology in cars. Utilizing drone technology, they provide Level 2 autonomy at a mere 5,000 yuan per vehicle, making it feasible even for cars priced around 100,000 yuan. This starkly contrasts with competitors like Huawei, whose systems are usually integrated into more expensive models.

At least 20 new models this year will incorporate DJI's technology, which employs cameras and affordable semiconductors instead of expensive lidar and radar systems. This method, inspired by DJI's Phantom 4 drone technology, enables vehicles to adjust their direction and speed based on camera inputs.

The attraction of DJI Automotive's technology is its cost-effectiveness, which could expedite the adoption of driver assistance systems. China, emphasizing such technology, targets Level 2 and 3 vehicles to account for 70% of new sales by 2030, with Level 4 vehicles comprising 20%.

DJI Automotive intends to launch a more advanced system by the end of the year, capable of self-driving on regular roads, priced at 7,000 yuan per vehicle. This initiative could further democratize autonomous driving technology, making it accessible to a wider market.

Level 2 autonomy pertains to systems that can manage both steering and acceleration/deceleration under specific conditions, necessitating the driver to monitor the environment and be prepared to intervene. Level 4 autonomy allows the vehicle to handle all driving aspects in certain conditions without human intervention.

### NVIDIA Surpasses Apple in Market Cap, Reaching $3 Trillion

Summary:
On June 5, NVIDIA, a leading tech company, surpassed Apple in market capitalization, reaching $3 trillion. This milestone positions NVIDIA as the second-largest U.S. company by market value. Analysts attribute this surge to NVIDIA's strong performance in the stock market and its strategic positioning in the rapidly growing field of artificial intelligence. In contrast, Apple's market performance has been relatively stagnant, partly due to its slower integration of AI technologies.

NVIDIA's upcoming stock split, converting one share into ten, is expected to make the stock more accessible to individual investors, potentially boosting its market presence further.

Explanation:
- Market Capitalization: The total value of all a company's shares of stock. It is calculated by multiplying the price of a stock by the total number of outstanding shares.
- Stock Split: A corporate action where a company divides its existing shares into multiple shares to boost the stock's liquidity. Although the number of shares increases by a specific multiple, the total dollar value of the shares remains the same compared to pre-split amounts, except for adjustments in the stock price to reflect the split.

Insights:
NVIDIA's meteoric rise underscores the market's appetite for companies at the forefront of technological innovation, particularly in AI. The stock split, by making shares more affordable, could democratize investment in such high-growth sectors, potentially influencing broader market dynamics and investor behavior.

### Smart and Connected Vehicles Gain Traction in China, Stocks Soar

<img

Summary:

The integration of smart technology into vehicles is accelerating. By 2025, 75.9% of new cars in China will be equipped with built-in internet connectivity, according to forecasts. This surge is reflected in the stock market, with companies like Hongquan Material and Zhonghai Da experiencing significant gains.

The government is actively supporting this shift. The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, along with other agencies, has approved nine consortia for pilot projects aimed at refining regulations and standards for smart vehicles. Additionally, a dedicated pool of 100 million phone numbers has been allocated for future car-to-everything (C-V2X) communications.

This move towards smart and connected vehicles is not just about convenience; it's a foundational shift in how we interact with transportation. As these technologies mature, they promise safer, more efficient travel, and could redefine the automotive industry's landscape.

Explanation of Terms:

- **Smart or Connected Vehicles:** Cars that use internet connectivity and on-board sensors to enhance safety, navigation, and other functionalities.
- **C-V2X (Cellular Vehicle-to-Everything):** Technology that allows vehicles to communicate with other vehicles (V2V), infrastructure (V2I), pedestrians (V2P), and network (V2N).
- **L3 Autonomous Driving:** A level of autonomy where the vehicle can handle most driving tasks but requires human intervention in certain situations.

At Computex 2024, the tech industry's top executives, including Intel's Pat Gelsinger, AMD's Lisa Su, and Qualcomm's Cristiano Amon, proclaimed AI as the driving force behind a PC renaissance. This transformation is set to invigorate a previously stagnant market, with AI-optimized PCs ready to deliver advanced capabilities such as real-time translation and AI assistants, which were once reliant on cloud services.

Competition is fierce among Intel, AMD, and the emerging Qualcomm. Intel's Lunar Lake chipset is capable of 48 trillion operations per second (TOPS), while AMD's Ryzen AI 300 series boasts 50 TOPS, both crucial benchmarks for AI processing power. Qualcomm, traditionally known for smartphone chips, is making a significant move into the laptop market with its Snapdragon X Elite chip, which meets Microsoft's AI PC requirement of over 40 TOPS.

The industry is transitioning from the traditional X86 architecture, which is performance-oriented, to Arm-based processors, which are known for their energy efficiency. Qualcomm forecasts that half of PCs will adopt Arm architecture, a shift that is being expedited by Microsoft's AI platform, Copilot+. Apple's transition to Arm for MacBooks is a prime example of this trend, already holding 11% of the market.

As AI remodels the PC landscape, the race is heating up to determine who will lead in this new era of computing. The outcome will not only reshape the market leaders but also redefine the essence of personal computing.

### Ubuntu Core 24 Released: Optimized for IoT and Embedded Devices

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/06/Ar-KjD-1717673952-0.jpg"
  alt="Ubuntu Core 24 Released: Optimized for IoT and Embedded Devices"
/>

Canonical has released Ubuntu Core 24, a fully containerized version of Ubuntu 24.04 LTS designed specifically for IoT and embedded devices. This release supports a variety of hardware, including Raspberry Pi, Qualcomm DragonBoard, Intel NUC, and devices based on x86_64 and RISC-V architectures.

Ubuntu Core 24 features a fully preemptible kernel, improving responsiveness and enabling advanced real-time capabilities on certified hardware. Key features include customizable image verification sets, enhanced control over automatic network requests, and improved GPU integration for AIoT devices and graphical applications. It also supports Ubuntu Frame for embedded Linux security and integrates with Canonical Landscape for device management.

This version seamlessly integrates with Azure IoT Edge and supports the Robot Operating System (ROS). Ubuntu Core 24 is a robust, embedded version of Ubuntu, designed for next-generation smart devices, especially those in the AIoT and edge computing sectors. It offers a complete AI stack with a real-time kernel, essential for making critical decisions in industrial, automotive, and security-sensitive embedded solutions.

Minimum system requirements are 384MB of RAM and 512MB of storage, with higher requirements for UEFI secure boot and FED. The release supports numerous devices, ensuring broad applicability.
