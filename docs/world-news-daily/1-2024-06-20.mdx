## UN Deputy Secretary General Amina Mohammed Calls for Increased Women's Representation in Global Leadership

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/Pol-DRS-1718866058-0.jpg"

/>

Amina Mohammed, UN Deputy Secretary General, expresses concern over the scarcity of women in global decision-making roles, linking the stagnation in conflict resolution and human development to this underrepresentation. She asserts that women bring a vital perspective absent from current power structures. Although there has been some progress, including the recent elections of female leaders in Iceland and Mexico, the global political arena remains predominantly male. Mohammed advocates for a reevaluation of strategies to empower women, stressing the importance of women leading these initiatives themselves, rather than depending on superficial solutions like affirmative action. The urgency of her message is highlighted by the UN's estimation that gender equality is over 300 years away at the current rate of progress.

### New Discovery of Immune Cells: Mobile Tregs May Treat Various Diseases

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/Hea-9Jp-1718866239-0.jpg"
  alt="New Discovery of Immune Cells: Mobile Tregs May Treat Various Diseases"
/>

New research from Cambridge University reveals that regulatory T cells (Tregs), known for suppressing immune responses, are not confined to specific body areas but are mobile, capable of healing various ailments. Tregs, a type of white blood cell, typically prevent overactive immune reactions, controlling inflammation and autoimmune diseases.

Traditionally viewed as stationary, Tregs are now understood to move throughout the body, offering localized healing. This discovery suggests potential for targeted treatments, avoiding the broad immunosuppression of current therapies.

The study, led by Adrian Liston, demonstrates the ability to attract Tregs to specific body parts using a developed drug, enhancing local healing and immune regulation. This approach could revolutionize treatments for conditions like multiple sclerosis and certain infectious diseases, focusing on localized immune suppression and tissue repair.

Liston and team aim to advance this research through human clinical trials, potentially transforming medical treatment paradigms. This breakthrough underscores the adaptability and critical role of Tregs in maintaining and restoring health.

### Baseball Legend Willie Mays Passes Away at 93

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/Spo-Jc1-1718866281-0.jpg"
  alt="Baseball Legend Willie Mays Passes Away at 93"
/>

Willie Mays, known as the "Say Hey Kid," has passed away at the age of 93. A towering figure in baseball, Mays captivated audiences with his power, speed, and unparalleled defensive skills. His illustrious career spanned 22 seasons, starting with the New York Giants, continuing with the San Francisco Giants, and concluding with a brief stint with the Mets.

Mays was a quintessential "five-tool" player: he excelled in hitting for average, hitting with power, base running, fielding, and throwing. His impressive .301 batting average, 660 home runs, and 3,293 hits highlight his exceptional abilities. Defensively, his record of 7,112 putouts and 12 Gold Glove awards are a testament to his prowess.

His most legendary moment occurred during the 1954 World Series with "the catch"—a spectacular over-the-shoulder grab that continues to amaze. Mays' influence transcended the field, as he was a pivotal figure in the integration of Major League Baseball and a recipient of the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

Mays' legacy is deeply ingrained in baseball history, serving as a beacon of excellence and a symbol of progress. His profound impact on the game and society stands as a testament to his enduring greatness.

### Innovative Smart Packaging Detects Food Contamination

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/06/Foo-DHb-1718866317-0.jpg"
  alt="Innovative Smart Packaging Detects Food Contamination"
/>

A team at McMaster University has developed smart food packaging capable of detecting contamination, with the aim of reducing food waste and preventing disease. Although the cost increase per package is minimal, widespread adoption by food producers has been slow due to concerns about consumer resistance to higher prices. Researchers contend that the long-term savings from decreased foodborne illnesses and waste could amount to billions globally, surpassing the initial costs.

Challenges encompass regulatory overhauls and consumer acceptance. Current date labeling practices are criticized for their excessive caution, resulting in significant waste. Since 2018, the team has introduced various technologies, including color-changing films and integrated lab-on-a-package devices, targeting common pathogens such as Listeria, Salmonella, and E. coli.

Transitioning from traditional calendar-based systems to these advanced detection methods necessitates collaboration with regulators and industry leaders. The objective is to implement a more precise, consumer-friendly approach to food safety.

Insight: The hesitation to adopt new technologies, even when advantageous, frequently arises from short-term economic concerns and established practices. Promoting a shift towards these innovations demands clear communication of their benefits and a collaborative approach involving all stakeholders.
