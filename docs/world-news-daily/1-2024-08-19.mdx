### Ancient Kelp Forests: A New Insight into Marine Ecosystems

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/08/Soc-jGz-1724065712-2.jpg"
  alt="Ancient Kelp Forests: A New Insight into Marine Ecosystems"
/>

New research reveals ancient kelp forests off the Pacific coast date back 3200 million years, older than previously thought. These forests supported extinct beaked whales before the arrival of modern sea otters and birds.

Kelp forests are complex ecosystems, vital for numerous marine species. The study uncovered evidence in 3200-year-old kelp holdfast fossils, indicating that kelp sustained ancient marine life, such as the now-extinct double-toothed whale.

This discovery challenges earlier beliefs that kelp forests evolved alongside modern marine fauna around 1400 million years ago. It suggests that kelp was present much earlier, laying the foundation for subsequent ecosystems.

The research, published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, utilized X-ray scans to analyze fossils. These scans revealed ancient kelp holdfasts, the root-like structures anchoring kelp to the sea floor, complete with attached shellfish and barnacles.

This study not only extends the timeline of kelp forest existence but also emphasizes the intricate evolution of marine ecosystems. It highlights how life adapts and evolves in the ocean, often in more complex ways than previously understood.

### New drug triggers ferroptosis in cancer cells, potentially inhibiting tumor growth.

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/08/Hea-57A-1724065887-0.jpg"
  alt="New drug triggers ferroptosis in cancer cells, potentially inhibiting tumor growth."
/>

The team from Bochum University has discovered a new drug that can trigger ferroptosis in cancer cells. Ferroptosis is a form of cell death characterized by the accumulation of lipid peroxides, catalyzed by iron. The team synthesized a cobalt-containing metal complex that accumulates in the mitochondria, producing reactive oxygen species that attack fatty acids, thereby inducing ferroptosis. Studies show that this substance can slow tumor growth, but it currently lacks the ability to selectively target tumor cells, requiring further research and improvement.

Ferroptosis: A form of cell death distinct from apoptosis, characterized by the accumulation of lipid peroxides.

Mitochondria: The energy factories within cells, involved in various cellular functions.

Reactive Oxygen Species: Highly reactive oxygen molecules that participate in cellular signaling and damage.

### Climate and Shipping Issues Drive Coffee Price Volatility

<img
  src="https://image.informedainews.com/2024/08/Foo-usj-1724065947-0.jpg"
  alt="Climate and Shipping Issues Drive Coffee Price Volatility"
/>

Coffee prices are on the rise, influenced by adverse weather conditions, shipping delays, and new environmental regulations. Robusta beans reached an all-time high in July, trading above $4,000 per ton, while Arabica beans are not far behind, at over $2 per pound.

Vietnam's coffee production has decreased due to El Nino, exacerbating previous shortages in Brazil. Shipping challenges and the EU's new deforestation regulations have further tightened the market.

Analysts foresee market volatility continuing through 2024, with some relief expected in 2025 as Brazil's harvests improve. However, climate change poses a significant threat to future production and quality.

In Vietnam, irregular rainfall has affected the size of coffee beans, complicating the harvesting and sorting processes. Rising temperatures and an increase in pests are also adding to farmers' difficulties and increasing costs.

The quest for cheaper beans has altered market dynamics, with robusta often driving price movements. Despite potential short-term price drops, structural issues suggest that coffee prices will likely remain higher than before.
